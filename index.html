A Dog's Life - An In-Depth Look at Major Aspects of the American Dog's Life

With the help of the Dog Age Project (DAP), we have access to a relatively large and novel dataset that provides similar healthcare data as you would find in the human world. This includes periodic checkups that gives us a comprehensive health history on the dog participants that includes ongoing health metrics that can be compared over time, diagnosis in the case of disease (including an extensive dataset tracking cancer occurrences), treatment data, physical and chemical environments, breed, age, gender, neuter status, behavior, diet, and the types and amount of physical activity. Utilizing this dataset, we take an analytical look at 3 key aspects of a dogs life: how owner demographics affect a dog's health, cancer trajectory, and the impact of spaying/neutering.
